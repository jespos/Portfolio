Overview

The Problem

Volkswagen enthusiasts have a variety of ways to access information: from online forums and Facebook groups for asking questions and posting photos, to personal cloud storage for storing car-related documents. But, no cohesive app currently exists where Volkswagen enthusiasts can store and share their passion from one platform.

The Solution

V-DUBS is free storage and sharing app for Volkswagen enthusiasts to share photos, tell stories, upload documents related to your passion, and ask questions to the community.

In-depth interviews:

Cars and Coffee

I had coffee with several members of the Volkswagen community to learn more about their web habits as they pertain to Volkswagen. I learned there are 3 major tasks that Volkswagen enthusiasts do:

  1. Share and view photos of Volkswagens
  2. Ask questions to the community
  3. Upload / share car-related paperwork and documents

Lesson: collecting more data isn't bad.

The addition of a second user survey set me back on my timeline. But, I felt that gathering additional data (especially after analyzing the results of the first survey) in order to create an app that the community wants to use was well worth the extra week and a half.

User Personas

Many people. Common Motivations.

When I attend Volkswagen events, I observe several types of people. Using my own observations, along with survey datal previously discussed, I generated 3 personas:

  1. Enthusiasts
  2. Creatives (i.e. photographers)
  3. Learners (i.e. car restoration)

Marco

The Learner | 27 years old

Motivations:

I love cars and am currently restoring a 1996 Volkswagen Jetta. I want to easily share restoration photos and ask questions to the community.

Frustrations:

I find myself sharing content in multiple places. I don’t want to use multiple apps to find and share information.

Brett

The Enthusiast | 65 years old

Motivations:

I consider myself an amateur car restorer, collector, and photographer. I want an app where I can share my passion with others who feel the same.

Frustrations:

I can’t find any cohesive apps related to this community. The other platforms I use are cluttered with irrelevant information.

Ellie

The Creative | 34 years old

Motivations:

I manage automobile exhibits for an antique auto museum and enjoy driving my classic VW to auto shows. I want an easy way to create notes and share photos in one app.

Frustrations:

Uploading my notes to share with others isn’t easy and I can’t create new content with my current apps.

User Stories

Less is more.

After researching apps and creating a lengthy list of tasks a user wants to do on the app, I prioritized tasks to create an MVP.

High Priority Items

  • Sign up for new account
  • Sign into account
  • Create content
  • View content
  • Upload content
  • Comment on content
  • Share content

Lesson: ‘Important’ functionality ≠ ‘high’ priority

The mistake I originally made was including too many high priority user stories. I decided to re-evaluate each user story using the 80/20 rule. Because a priority isn’t set to high, does not mean I won’t develop it. It will just be developed with less urgency.

Visual Design

Branding

Don't let simplicity scare you.

Volkswagen is a playful brand with vibrant vehicle colors and humorous, vintage car ads it was important for V-DUBS to incorporate these values into the brand. I want the target audience to identify with the elements of the app: from the logo design and color palette to typography and imagery.

Logo

From word cloud to V-DUBS

The logo evolved from a word cloud of car-related terms to the final version of a Volkswagen Beetle. The logo needed to be clear and easily identifiable. There is almost nothing else more identifiable than the shape of a Volkswagen Beetle.

The name, V-DUBS, is a play on letters V-W. I played with other names, but quickly settled on this as it is an identifiable term in the VW community. The font itself resembles the font found on European license plates.

Typography

What's your type?

FE Font is a display font that mimics the font used on European license plates it is used only for the logo. Lato is a simple, modern font to complement the playfulness of Fe Font and is used for all headlines and text throughout the app.

User Testing Round 2

I put my prototype through a second round of user testing going through 14 tasks.

View Tasks

Results Summary:

  1. There is a search bar and a search icon. Are they both needed?
  2. Replacing or deleting a photo / document isn’t obvious.
  3. Sign Out should be more prominently displayed and easier to find.

Lesson: You wont catch everything the first go around

When moving from user testing round 1 to my hi-fi mockups, I noticed several tasks that I should have tested for. They were incorporated into the second round of user testing.

Conclusion

In the Volkswagen community, everyone has a story. V-DUBS is a platform for every VW lover and enthusiast to share their story. Being a Volkswagen enthusiast myself, this project was fun to bring from concept to completion. Though I expected my initial timeline to be shorter, encountering and dealing with these problems in the beginning (i.e. a second user survey to collect more data) made for 1) less assumptions about what users want and 2) an accurate app representing the wants and needs of the community. One of my major challenges was containing / limiting scope creep. I have so many interesting ideas for what the app could be in the future. So, I had to resist the urge to do all the things at once with the app.
